J.R.R. Tolkien

The man who dreamt and changed fiction

Here's a timeline of Tolkien's life:

  • 1892 - (Jan) Born in Bloemfontein, Orange Free State
  • 1896 - (Feb) Father passed away so moved to Kings Heath, Birmingham
  • 1904 - (Nov) Mother passed away
  • 1911 - (Aug) Took a summer holiday in Switzerland
  • 1911 - (Oct) Began studying at Exeter College, Oxford
  • 1914 - (Aug) Britain entered the First World War
  • 1915 - Graduated with first-class honours degree in English language and literature
  • 1915 - (Jul) Commissioned as a temporary second lieutenant in the Lancashire Fusiliers
  • 1916 - (Mar) Married to Edith Mary Bratt in Warwick
  • 1916 - (June) Posted in France
  • 1916 - (Oct) Fought in the Battle of Somme and contracted trench fever and began alternating between hospitals and garrison duties
  • 1917 - (Nov) John Francis Reuel Tolkien, first of four children, was born
  • 1918 - (Jan) Promoted to the temporary rank of lieutenant and stationed at Kingston upon Hull, where his wife was allowed to live with him briefly
  • 1918 - (Nov) Returned to Oxford and employed with the New English Dictionary [Oxford English Dictionary]
  • 1919 - (Jul) Taken from active service
  • 1920 - Worked as a Reader in English Language at Leeds University
  • 1925 - Took up an appointment as Rawlinson and Bosworth Professor of Anglo-Saxon at Oxford
  • 1926 - Translated Beowulf though did not publish it
  • 1936 - Seminal lecture (later published as an article) of Beowulf
  • 1937 - (Sep) Published The Hobbit
  • 1945 - Became Oxford's Merton Professor of English Language and Literature
  • 1947 - "On Fairy-Stories" was published in Essays Presented to Charles Williams
  • 1948 - Completed The Lord of the Rings
  • 1954 - Published The Fellowship of the Ring, first of the three books
  • 1971 - (Nov) Edith Tolkien passed away
  • 1973 - (Sep) Passed away in Bournemouth, Hampshire, England
